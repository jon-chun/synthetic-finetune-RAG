Positional Encoding Analogy

This animation shows how positional encoding adds information about sequence order. Each position in the sequence (represented by the vertical axis of the waves, advancing over time) gets a unique encoding vector. Here, we visualize 4 dimensions of that vector. Lower dimensions (left, Period T) vary rapidly, while higher dimensions (right, Period 8T) vary slowly. The 'bits' (0/1) show the sign of the sine wave for that dimension at the current position. Notice the pattern doesn't count 0-15 sequentially, but generates a unique signature for each position based on these frequencies.
